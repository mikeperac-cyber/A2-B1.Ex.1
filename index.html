The Remote-Work Experiment

Distinguish regular habits from temporary actions in a workplace article.

Grammar focusPresent Simple vs. Present ContinuousRemote work, focus, and distractions

01

Useful vocabulary

Listen, read the example, and say each word aloud.

distraction

something that takes attention away

“Phone alerts are a common distraction.”

productive

producing useful results

“I feel productive in a quiet room.”

flexible

able to change easily

“A flexible schedule helps parents.”

concentrate

to give full attention

“She concentrates better in the morning.”

boundary

a limit that separates activities

“He sets a boundary between work and rest.”

temporary

lasting for a limited time

“This is a temporary arrangement.”

02

Grammar in a nutshell

Form

Habit: subject + base verb. Now/temporary: am/is/are + -ing.

Use

Use the Present Simple for regular patterns and the Present Continuous for current or temporary situations.

Example

Nora usually works downtown, but this month she is working from home.

Watch out

State verbs such as know, need, and prefer do not usually take the continuous form.

Warm-up

Connect the idea

Do you concentrate better at home or in a shared workplace? Give one reason.

03

Today’s practice

Read the article, then answer the questions.

Nora usually works in a busy downtown office. She takes the train each morning and starts at 8:30. This month, however, her company is testing a flexible remote-work plan. Nora is working from home three days a week. She enjoys the quiet mornings, but she sometimes misses quick conversations with her team. To stay productive, she turns off phone notifications and takes a short walk at lunchtime. Today she is using a shared workspace because builders are repairing the apartment upstairs. The experiment is teaching Nora an important lesson: a good routine matters wherever you work.

1. Where does Nora normally work?

2. Why is she using a shared workspace today?

3. Which situation is temporary?

4. What helps Nora stay productive?

5. What is the article’s main message?
